The mid-book wall

A novel is too big to hold in one head.

Somewhere around 40,000 words, the project stops fitting in your memory — and the tools fight you.

🗂️

Twelve scattered places

Manuscript in one app, research in tabs, character notes in a notebook, outline on a whiteboard. Nothing knows about anything else.

🔁

Continuity rot

Her eyes were green in chapter two. The journey took three days, then somehow four. You only catch it when a reader does.

📋

Copy-paste AI

The chatbot drafts in its own window. You paste it in, lose the formatting, lose the thread, and it never saw chapter nine.

🌫️

The sagging middle

You know what happens in act one and the ending. The 30,000 words between them are fog — and no outline survives them.
