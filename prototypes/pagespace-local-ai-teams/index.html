The wall every solo agent hits

A great agent on one laptop is still a silo of one.

You love that it acts on its own. But the moment a second person needs in — or you get tired of being the one keeping the box alive at 2am — the magic stops, because all of it lives on your machine.

✕

The brain lives on one machine

Memory, skills, and everything the agent learned about your stack are trapped on your box. A teammate gets a blank bot and starts from zero.

✕

Handoff means copy-paste

The agent does great work, then you paste it into a chat thread. The thread becomes the deliverable — nobody can edit it, search it, or build on it.

✕

You're the one keeping it alive

Someone has to babysit the process, watch the spend, and patch it. The agent is only as available as your laptop — and only you can fix it when it falls over.

How it works

Keep the agent. Lose the single point of failure.

PageSpace isn't a chatbot you copy answers out of. It's a shared cloud workspace where agents do the work inside the team's documents, sheets, and tasks — using real tools, with everyone watching it happen and nothing to run.

Spin up a shared drive and write the context once

Create a team drive and set its standing context — your stack, conventions, and runbooks — with update_drive_context. Every agent and every teammate reads from the same source instead of re-explaining the world to a fresh bot.
